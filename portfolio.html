Experience and Goals

In my experience with Computer Science, I have taken classes such as Intro to Program Design and Concepts, Discrete Structures of Computing (Discrete Math), Data Structures and Algorithms, Computer Organization, and Programming Languages, and am currently taking Intro to Computer Systems and Programming Studio. These classes have taught me the basics of C++, Scheme, and Java, as well as given me background in program and algorithm design and analysis, processor design, and the basics of how programming languages operate and communicate with the processor. Along with the languages previously mentioned, I have some experience working with C# and Python, although not as extensive as my experience with the others. Outside of school, I have worked as an intern in the IT department for Bioworld Merchandising in Dallas, Texas. This has provided me with experience of working as part of a team in a real world working environment and communicating technical information with people in other departments with less technical experience and knowledge, as well more general problem solving skills. My goal with Computer Science is to learn as much as I can and use my experience in the real world to make an impact on the world. While most of my experience at this point is in the classroom, so many different parts of society today rely heavily on computers and present me endless opportunity to translate the knowledge I have and will accumulate to something that will allow me to help people, even in a way that may not be visible to most people. As of right now, my interests lie primarily in the fields of software engineering and cybersecurity, although operating systems and AI also interest me. Further into my college career, I will be taking courses in all of these disciplines, giving me background knowledge to learn and adapt to anything that I am needed to do.
