My Service Experience



In high school, I assisted teachers in the classroom at the Notre Dame School in Dallas, a school for children with special needs. Here, I assisted in the classroom by helping the teachers facilitate activities through leading small groups and helping the kids learn math. After class, I ate lunch and talked with the kids from my class, then went to recess with them to play and provide them with a friendly face every Wednesday that cared about them and their development.





In college, I participated in The Big Event in 2019. This is an event that takes place every year where students help out at various different sites in the Bryan/College Station area. When I participated, a group of students from my student organization and I went to the house of a local family to weed and mulch their yard, beautifying the neighborhood and making it a more pleasant place to live.



As a sophomore, I joined SLIDE (Sophomore Leaders Impacting Developing and Educating), a sophomore leadership organization with a focus on raising awareness for mental health and sexual assault. I was a member of the mental health awareness subcommittee, RAMH (Raising Awareness for Mental Health), in which we put on various mental health awareness themed events as a part of Mental Health Week in March, such as an open mic night and trivia night. As a junior I am now a Director on staff for RAMH, for which I help the sophomores plan events like those we put on as sophomores ourselves. I also serve as a friend and role model to them to help them grow as leaders and people.
